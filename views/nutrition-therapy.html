Procedures For Sick Animals

We are not veterinarians and what we do can by no means replace a visit with the vet. If your horse or dog is ill or shows signs that could potentially be related to illness or other medical conditions, always contact your veterinarian first.If your horse or dog is on medication, you must inform it before any consultation as we do not massage or do any other physical balancing on sick animals

The root of nutritional problems

As a Veterinary Nutrition Therapist , you work with the knowledge that the body constantly builds, breaks down, excretes and absorbs to ensure that it stays as healthy and healthy as possible.

Bring your Dogs, Horses or Cats and we will take care of your problem.

Nutritional therapy can help with

  • Optimal nutrition, vitamins, minerals, fat, protein, carbohydrate distribution, raw materials and their effect
  • Supporting for allergy, skin, hooves and fur problems
  • Behavioral problems (eg anxiety, stress, over / under energy etc.)
  • Support for joints and locomotives
  • Supporting digestive and intestinal issues
  • Over / underweight
