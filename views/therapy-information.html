Behavioral problems cannot be ignored

Many behavioral and training problems, in both dogs and horses, arise from muscle or joint problems. It can be anything from less muscle tension to reduced joint mobility. In addition to this, pain in all shadows can cause problems or changes in the animal's behavioral and reaction pattern.

Physical surgical therapy for horses and dogs is performed by Majken, Susanne and Bettina. We each have our specialty so you and your animal will meet the one of us who can best help you and your animal.

In addition to behavioral symptoms of physical problems, we often see examples of horses and dogs

  • not going righ
  • acting "stiff"
  • have a "bad side"
  • dysplasia
  • suffered an injury

Physical therapy with us covers a combination of several physiotherapy techniques:

  • Massage

    When working with physical therapy, we never use a therapy form alone. We combine the various tools and thus adapt the techniques to fit your horse or dog optimally.

    The massage techniques we use are based on "Intelligent Touch" and the traditional Swedish massage. In addition to this we use trigger points, stress points, special massage, pressure points and pain points.

    In addition, we are inspired by "holistic massage" for people, where we look at the different muscles from a viewpoint of traditional Chinese medicine.

    With this technique we can increase blood circulation , increase elasticity of muscles, tendons and ligaments and together prevents injury and promotes performance

  • Cranio Sacral Therapy

    is a gentle influence on the animal's body, which is gradually known, widely used and recognized when it comes to treating humans. In recent years, however, more and more have also caught the eye of how effective this balancing form is for animals.This techniques can be performed on the entire body. They are gentle, do not hurt, but are often very effective.

    We work with gentle stretches and pressures that relax the bones in relation to each other, thus also the muscles, as well as the cero spinal fluid and the meninges

    We use KST to balance problems in the moving device, muscle tension , problems in bone and muscle system.

  • Joint mobilization

    We work with gentle techniques, not harmful ones, and are happy to refer to a veterinarian or a chiropractor who has a different and deeper education than ours if there is a suspicion of problems that require different examination or more powerful techniques.

    Many muscle tensions come from imbalances and impaired movement of the animal's joints. This can be about all joints in the body, but the joints we can typically affect are in the hip, pelvis, back, loins and neck area.

    Joint mobilization is always supplemented with massage and also with KST to relax related muscles.

    If the massage is not sufficient, other aids, such as the influence of laser acupuncture points, may be included in the balancing.

  • Laser Therapy

    At Center for Animal Therapy we have two types of laser equipment for horses and dogs. Laser therapy works by sending a special type of light into the cells. When the laser light hits the cells that are not working optimally, the body optimizes its own cleansing and healing process.

    We know that the laser light penetrates deep into the cells it strikes. The cells transform the light into ATP, which acts as energy for the cells, thereby getting more energy to perform their work - for example, a healing process in connection with a wound, cleansing a muscle or reducing inflammation in a joint.

    Laser has several beneficial properties - pain-reducing, increases blood flow, stimulates drainage, relaxing effect on muscles.

    We have the latest technology in laser equipment. A powerful Class IV laser from LightCure, which can be set specifically for your dog or horse. Furthermore, we have a smaller Class 3B laser from PowerMedic for several superficial problems.

  • Thermography

    We offer thermographic check of your horse or dog in connection with physical therapy.

    Before we can begin to address any physical challenges, it is important to deal specifically with the imbalances in the body. This is usually done through a combination of gait analysis, motion test and palpation.In some cases, the x-ray or scan - referral to a veterinarian is needed before we know for sure what is wrong

    Thermography is a relatively new technology where we can take a picture of the animal with a special heat sensitive camera. Heat fluctuations around joints, muscles or specific areas may indicate that something is not as it should be.
